Tender 10-Point Scoring System

The South African government uses a comprehensive 10-point scoring system to evaluate tender applications fairly and transparently. Understanding this system is crucial for successful tender applications.

Scoring System Overview

Tenders are evaluated based on a 100-point scale distributed across 10 key criteria. Each criterion is weighted according to its importance for the specific tender category.

The 10 Evaluation Criteria

1

Price

Competitive pricing relative to project scope and market rates.

Weight: 20-30 points

2

B-BBEE Status

Broad-Based Black Economic Empowerment compliance level.

Weight: 10-20 points

3

Technical Capability

Demonstrated ability to execute the project successfully.

Weight: 15-25 points

4

Experience

Relevant experience in similar projects.

Weight: 10-15 points

5

Financial Stability

Financial capacity to undertake and complete the project.

Weight: 5-10 points

6

Methodology

Proposed approach and methodology for project execution.

Weight: 10-15 points

7

Local Content

Use of local resources, materials, and labor.

Weight: 5-10 points

8

Timeline

Realistic and efficient project timeline.

Weight: 5-10 points

9

Risk Management

Identification of risks and mitigation strategies.

Weight: 5-10 points

10

Innovation

Innovative approaches and solutions proposed.

Weight: 5-10 points

Tips for Improving Your Score

Price Competitiveness

Research market rates thoroughly and ensure your pricing is competitive while maintaining profitability.

B-BBEE Compliance

Ensure your B-BBEE certification is current and at the highest possible level for your business.

Technical Documentation

Provide comprehensive technical documentation that clearly demonstrates your capability to execute the project.

Portfolio Presentation

Present your previous experience with detailed case studies, outcomes, and client testimonials.

Financial Statements

Include audited financial statements that demonstrate your company's stability and capacity.

Detailed Methodology

Develop a clear, detailed methodology that addresses all aspects of the project requirements.
